About The Project

This tool was created for a group of UK hospitality workers who found themselves in an impossible position: they trusted their employer to handle their pay, and pension contributions correctly, only to discover that trust had been badly abused. What happened to them isn't rare, and it should never fall on workers to become forensic accountants just to understand their own payslips.

This project exists because they deserved better.

What this tool does

The Anonymous Payroll Reporter helps workers check whether their employer has been paying the correct pension contributions. It analyses payroll data and pension spreadsheets, highlights discrepancies, and produces a clear, worker‑friendly report you can use to understand what’s going on.

It’s designed for people who don’t have HR departments, legal teams, or accountants behind them—just the right to be paid fairly.

Can I try it out without uploading my own files?

Yes. We provide a small training pack of dummy payslips and pension files so you can learn how the tool works. Download, unzip, and upload the files to follow along with the walkthrough. Download sample files

Will this work for me?

It depends on the payroll system your employer uses. The tool currently supports Sage Payroll and Nest Pensions, and it works best when your payslip follows the standard layout used by those systems.

Because employers often customise their payslips—even when using the same payroll software—some layouts may not be recognised. If your payslip looks noticeably different from the example below, the tool may not interpret it correctly. The project is keen to expand support for additional payroll systems, so please let us know if you find yourself in this situation.

To check compatibility, compare your payslip with the sample layout shown here. If the structure and labels are similar, the tool should work as expected. If not, you may see incomplete or inaccurate results.

Privacy & Safety

Your data never leaves your device. All calculations happen entirely in your browser. Nothing is uploaded, nothing is stored, and nothing is sent to any server. You can close the tab and walk away knowing your information stays with you.

The code is fully open source and licensed under MIT, so anyone can inspect, audit, or improve it.

Why it’s anonymous

Workers deserve tools that protect them. Many people cannot safely raise concerns about payroll errors or pension underpayments without risking retaliation. This tool gives you a way to understand the facts first, privately and safely, before deciding what to do next.

How to use this app

Step 1: Prep

A) Payslips & Holiday Forms

What to gather
  • From your email, download all payslips PDFs for the dates you want to review (ideally for your entire employment).
  • Also gather copies of all holiday forms so you can assess what holiday pay you should have received in a given month.
Note: Ask your manager if you need a password to open your payslips, or if you are missing any dates.

B) NEST contributions

Logging into NEST
  • If you've never logged into NEST, first create your account here: Sign up for NEST
Note: Your NEST member number can be found on any letter they've sent - it looks like this:
MEM048559238
What to download

Nest provides a pension contributions record for each financial year.

After logging in visit: NEST: Contribution history

  1. Select a Tax Year

    You will repeat this process for each financial year.

  2. Select current Employer

    Select employer (current company name, if more than one).

  3. Tick both contribution types

    Important: Choose both "From your salary" and "From your employer".

  4. Click 'Apply Filters' & 'Download'

    Click "Apply filters" and then the "Download Results" buttons.

Rinse and repeat - You will need to change the year and click "apply filters" and "download results" for each financial year.

Step 2: Select Documents

Select Documents

  • Enter your payroll document password if required
  • Select Payroll documents, and (optionally) pension documents

Submit

  • Check the disclaimer box
  • Click "Process Documents"

Step 3: Review & Report

A) Verify Tax Payments

If you have any concerns about your tax payments, you should verify them with HMRC - eg your payslip is missing deductions taxcode or National Insurance ID. You can do this by downloading the HMRC app from the Apple App or Google Play Stores.

B) Email your manager

If you note any discrepancies in your pension contributions, or deductions first email your manager with these concerns. You may wish to forward them a copy of this report as evidence.

C) Join a union

If you're not already a member, why not a union like Unite?

Note: Unions can advise, but often can't provide direct legal help for issues reported within four weeks of joining, so its best to join before you have an issue.
